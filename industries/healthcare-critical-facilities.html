The Reality

Critical Facility Fuel Challenges We Solve

Generator Fuel Level Unknown

Many facilities only know their generator fuel level when the low-fuel alarm triggers. By then, it is a scramble. Staff get pulled from patient care or core operations to manage a fuel emergency that should have been prevented.

Impact: Reactive operations. Staff distraction.

Fuel Quality Degradation

Standby generators sit for months between outages. Diesel degrades. Microbial growth contaminates tanks. When the generator finally needs to run, fuel quality issues cause failures at the worst possible moment.

Impact: Generator failure during outages.

Compliance Documentation Gaps

Healthcare facilities face survey requirements for emergency power systems. Accreditors expect documentation of fuel levels, testing schedules, and maintenance. Gaps create findings and remediation requirements.

Impact: Survey findings. Compliance burden.

Storm Response Scramble

Major storms are forecast days ahead, but fuel vendors are overwhelmed when everyone calls at once. Facilities that wait until the storm warning find their usual vendor overcommitted. Proactive pre-storm fills require a vendor relationship, not a transaction.

Impact: Fuel uncertainty during emergencies.
