Site Requirements & Safety Considerations

FuelCube placement requires attention to site conditions, regulatory setbacks, and operational safety. Fox Fuel Pro evaluates these factors during site assessment and provides clear guidance for compliant deployment.

Surface & Pad Requirements

  • Ground surface: Level, compacted soil or gravel. Asphalt or concrete pad recommended for permanent installations.
  • Drainage: Sloped away from tank to prevent water pooling. No standing water under FuelCube.
  • Load capacity: Surface must support FuelCube weight when full (500-gal: ~4,000 lbs; 1000-gal: ~8,000 lbs; 2000-gal: ~16,000 lbs).

Clearances, Setbacks & Access

  • Delivery access: Minimum 12-ft wide access path for delivery truck. Turning radius and overhead clearance verified during site survey.
  • Setbacks: Typical fire code requires 10-40 ft clearance from buildings, property lines, or ignition sources (varies by jurisdiction).
  • Operational clearance: Minimum 3-5 ft around FuelCube for hose access, pump cabinet operation, and maintenance.

Security & Fencing

  • Cabinet lock: FuelCube pump cabinet includes keyed lock to prevent unauthorized fueling.
  • Site security: Customer responsible for perimeter fencing or access control if required by site security policies.
  • Theft prevention: Optional monitoring systems provide anomaly detection for unusual fuel drawdowns.

Spill Containment, Venting & Fire Safety

  • Double-wall containment: FuelCube includes UL142 double-wall design with 110% secondary containment capacity—no separate containment required.
  • Spill kit: Absorbent pads and drip pan provided for minor spills during fueling operations.
  • Venting: Integrated pressure relief venting per UL142 standards. Vent positioned away from ignition sources.
  • Fire extinguisher: Recommended (not included). Customer responsible per OSHA and fire code requirements.

Note: Fox Fuel Pro provides compliance guidance based on our regional regulatory experience in PA, NJ, and DE. Final responsibility for permit compliance and authority having jurisdiction (AHJ) approvals rests with the customer. We assist with documentation and coordination but do not provide legal or engineering services.
